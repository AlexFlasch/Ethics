Ethics Scenario

by: Alex Flasch

Preface

You’ve just landed your dream job working at Connekt. They’ve got a fairly well established communication platform, and you’re an avid user yourself. Connekt has been promising to make the next generation in communication software. They aim to please the customer and to come up with innovative new features that allow users to connect with each other in ways that haven’t been explored before. Connekt hasn’t been around for too long, but they’ve already got quite a good reputation for responding to user feedback and listening to what their community wants.

Scenario

Walking into the office your first day it’s pretty easy to see that the office culture is extremely laid back. Everyone smiles and welcomes you to the team as you find your way to your desk. The guy the next desk over is named Jackson, and after holding a few-minute-long conversation with him, you find out that there’s even a beer fridge in the break room! Awesome! “Sounds like I’ll fit in just fine here” you decide. As you sit down at your desk your first day, you’re presented with some training. “This will probably take a few weeks to get through…” You think to yourself. And so you begin working.

A week later you’ve gotten through a good portion of the training required. Unfortunately the work is rather mundane. Staying hopeful, you tell yourself it’ll be more interesting once you get to work on the actual product. Jackson likes to talk a lot and has been making it hard to get through all of your training.

Finally, after what seems like eons, all of your training is complete. Your department lead has looked it over and she says everything looks fantastic! She assigns you your first features to work on and you excitedly begin implementing them.

It’s Jackson again. He wants to know how your work is going, but you know he’s just going to talk your ear off. Same as every other time he drops in. The boss walks by as you two are talking, and you try to look like you’re at least staying productive while Jackson continues to blabber. Jackson either doesn’t care, or doesn’t notice the boss walking by. Finally he leaves and you can get back to work.

After finishing all of the features that were assigned to you, you get to watch as they go live. You’re excited to hear the users’ opinions on them. You just hope that they keep up Connekt’s high standards and that the users enjoy them. Within a few days, though, you get some bug reports on your new features, so you set out on a bug hunting. In the middle of your bug-squashing session your boss walks in and tells you he’s got a new feature for you to work on. “It’s a doozy” she says. She asks you to implement platform-wide encryption on all forms of data sent through Connekt and all of their services. You love the idea, and even though you know you’ve got a long road ahead of you, you’re excited to get encryption working, and so you start typing away madly at your keyboard.

Another few weeks have passed and Jackson has since been “let go” from Connekt, and you can only assume it’s due to his lack of work ethic. He’s constantly going around chatting to all of the other developers, and you’re not sure you’ve even seen any commits from him in the last month or two. You’re a little sad to see him go, seeing as he’s the one that you’ve talked to most, but secretly you’re a bit relieved that you can be more productive and not have to worry too heavily about your boss walking by as Jackson is talking to you.

In the middle of putting some of the finishing touches on your encryption, your boss approaches you. “Hey! Great job so far with the encryption. I’ve been following it pretty closely since it’s a heavily requested feature from our users. Seeing as it’s almost done, I’d love to take you and a few of the other developers out to the bar to celebrate! Does tonight work for you?”

“Sure! I’d love to meet you guys there!” You reply.

Later that night, you meet up with your boss and all of your coworkers. As you sit down at the bar you order a rum and coke, your go-to drink. After a couple of drinks your boss pulls you aside.

“Hey, so I wanted to talk to you about a feature that we’ve been asked to implement in the encryption.”

“Sure, what kinda feature are we implementing?”

“Well, the government has demanded we put a backdoor in our encryption so that they can track any potentially threatening users that might be using our systems.”

So, how do you reply?
