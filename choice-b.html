Ethics Scenario

by: Alex Flasch

Choice B

“It really doesn’t sit well with me, and I’m not sure I can do that to our users. Isn’t there any way we could avoid implementing a backdoor in our system?” You ask.

“Unfortunately, since the government has requested this of us, we couldn’t easily refuse them. If we wanted to ignore it, it would likely take a large legal battle to settle, and Connekt just isn’t big enough to handle that right now. So, I’d like it if you just implemented it and hopefully we can figure something out later.”

You decide to ask your boss if there’s any way the company could avoid putting the backdoor into the encryption. Putting a master lock on your encryption that you’ve worked so hard on feels like it would dirty your achievements. On top of all that, the thought of having potential security holes being used by the government, hackers, or maybe even Connekt itself doesn’t sit well with you.

By asking your boss before making a final decision, you've honored section 1.4 of the ACM code, but you've still got another decision to make...
