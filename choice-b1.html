Ethics Scenario

by: Alex Flasch

Choice B1

“Alright. I’ll put the backdoor in, but I’d really prefer if we can find some way to not put it to use. I don’t particularly like the idea of a backdoor in our system, seeing as it can be used by others that it isn’t intended for.” You tell your boss.

“Thanks so much! I’m sure Connekt can find a way to fight for its removal at some point down the line!”

But somehow you doubt that Connekt will ever get to fighting the government on such a large issue being the small company it is. You even begin to wonder if the company intends to fight it at all. But you love your job, and a little bit of guilt is better than looking for another career, right?

As stated previously, by asking your boss about your options first, you honored section 1.4 of the ACM code. By implementing the backdoor, you've upheld section 2.6 of the ACM Code of Ethics: "Honor contracts, agreements, and assigned responsibilities", but goes against section 1.7 "Respect the privacy of others". While you're being a good employee by following orders, you allow another company to invade users' privacy.
