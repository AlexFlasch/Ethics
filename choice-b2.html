Ethics Scenario

by: Alex Flasch

Choice B2

“I’m not sure that I feel comfortable implementing the backdoor at all…” You trail off. “I really love working here, but putting something so easily abusable in our system really goes against my morals. I don’t think that I can do this.”

“I’m afraid that adding in that backdoor is critical to your job, so you’re going to have to do it, unless you’d rather look for a different job.” Your boss retorts, somewhat angrily.

“Well if that’s the case, I don’t think I can work here any longer. I’m sorry” you confess.

You feel better knowing that you held to your beliefs, but you’re a bit sad you won’t get to work at your dream job anymore. Luckily there are at least a few other jobs you can think of that you’d love to work at. Better get searching!

By refusing to implement the backdoor, but asking first you've upheld section 1.4 of the ACM code of ethics "Be fair and take action not to discriminate" You've also broken section 2.6, "Honor contracts, agreements and assigned responsibilities" by refusing to do your assigned work. But you've upheld section 1.7 "Respect the privacy of others" by refusing to implement a backdoor for the company.
